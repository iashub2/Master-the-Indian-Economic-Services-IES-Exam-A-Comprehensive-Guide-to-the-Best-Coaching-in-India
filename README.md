H2: IES Eligibility Criteria and Exam Pattern
Before starting your preparation, it is crucial to understand if you are eligible. The criteria are strict to ensure only subject-matter experts enter the service.
H3: Educational Qualification & Age Limit
Education: A Post-Graduation degree in Economics or Econometrics from a recognized university is mandatory. Final-year students of their Master's degree are also eligible to appear.
Age Limit: The candidate must be between 21 to 30 years of age.
Relaxation: Age relaxation is available for SC/ST (up to 35 years), OBC (up to 33 years), and PwD candidates (up to 40 years).
Nationality: Must be a citizen of India, or a subject of Nepal/Bhutan, or a Tibetan refugee who came to India before Jan 1st, 1962.
H3: The Written Examination Pattern
The written exam totals 1000 Marks, consisting of six papers. It is a mix of objective and descriptive questions.
Sr. No.	Subject	Marks	Duration
1	General English	100	Day 1
2	General Studies	100	Day 1
3	General Economics-I	200	Day 2
4	General Economics-II	200	Day 2
5	General Economics-III	200	Day 3
6	Indian Economics	200	Day 3
Candidates who qualify for the written stage are called for the Personality Test (Interview), which carries 200 Marks. This final stage assesses leadership qualities, intellectual curiosity, and suitability for the post.
